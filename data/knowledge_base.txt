Our platform uses horizontal autoscaling to handle sudden increases in request volume. A load balancer distributes traffic to stateless API pods, and scaling policies monitor CPU, memory, and queue depth. During high demand periods, new instances are launched in seconds, reducing latency spikes and preventing request drops.

Peak load protection is reinforced through asynchronous processing. Long-running tasks are pushed to a message queue, where worker pools consume jobs at a controlled rate. Backpressure rules slow ingestion when queue lag grows, protecting downstream databases from overload.

Caching is used at multiple layers. Frequently requested metadata is cached in-memory with short TTLs, while larger query results are stored in a distributed cache. Cache hit rates above eighty percent significantly reduce pressure on primary storage during traffic bursts.

The system includes circuit breakers and retry budgets for external dependencies. If a third-party API slows down, the circuit opens to avoid cascading failures. Requests may be served with stale but safe fallback data until upstream services recover.

Observability is built around metrics, traces, and structured logs. Dashboards track p95 latency, error rates, queue lag, and saturation. Alerting thresholds are tuned to detect early degradation and trigger auto-remediation playbooks.

For data consistency, writes use idempotency keys and transactional outbox patterns. This allows safe retries when transient errors happen under load. Event consumers apply deduplication checks so replayed events do not create duplicate records.

Disaster recovery relies on multi-zone deployment and automated failover. Replicas in secondary zones can absorb traffic if a primary zone degrades. Recovery point objectives are maintained through continuous replication and periodic restore drills.

Security controls remain active during scale events. Rate limiting and token validation are enforced at the edge to block abuse traffic. Secret rotation and least-privilege IAM policies reduce risk exposure during incident response.
